Recommended order

Field sequence

  1. Opening and machine identification
    Wide shot, nameplate, work area.
  2. Pre-start safety check
    Fuel spray prevention caps, ECM rubber mounts, loose clips, loose hardware.
  3. General pre-inspection
    Injector area, high-pressure pipes, hose routing, work area cleanliness.
  4. Fuel filter air bleeding
    Bleed prefilter first, then main filter. Confirm fuel flow without bubbles.
  5. Engine start
    Crank with monitor visible. Keep each crank within the allowed limit.
  6. Initial idle and leak check
    Fuel lines, injector area, coolant hoses, under-engine area.
  7. Monitor check
    Confirm no fault codes or abnormal warnings.
  8. Operation check
    Idle to high idle. Show smooth engine response.
  9. Closing confirmation
    Machine running normally, clean final wide shot.
Critical pass conditions

Do not proceed unless these are true

  • All fuel spray prevention caps are present and secure.
  • ECM rubber mounts are not cracked, hardened, or loose.
  • Fuel has been bled at both bleed points.
  • Fuel flow at bleed points is free of air bubbles.
  • No fuel or coolant leakage is visible after start.
  • No fault codes are shown on the machine monitor.
Bleed plug torque
7.8 to 9.8 Nm
Cranking note
Keep each crank within the stated limit. Show monitor and starter action clearly.

Important notes

Field reminders

  • Do the safety checks before engine start.
  • There are two bleed points: prefilter and main filter.
  • Film each critical step twice when possible: one wide shot and one close-up.
  • Do not rely on spoken audio alone. Engine noise will be strong, so captions matter.
  • Use portable lighting for engine-bay close-ups.
  • For leak checks, move slowly and hold the shot long enough to prove surfaces are dry.
  • If the engine does not start, recheck that bleeding was completed thoroughly.

NEW - Critical Validation

Injector & ECM Validation

  • Injector trim/compensation values written correctly
  • Confirmed via INSITE
  • No fault codes after write
Warning: Do NOT start engine before trim input
NEW - 4 Stage Leak Test

Fuel Leakage Test (Factory Method)

  • Apply dye to injector pipes, common rail, supply pump
  • Stage 1: Low idle → stop → inspect
  • Stage 2: High idle → stop → inspect
  • Stage 3: Load → stop → inspect
  • If leakage found → repair and restart
Run → Stop → Inspect (repeat)
